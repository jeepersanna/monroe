What to expect

Doors open at 5:00, food starts at 5:15. Plan to stay an hour or two — there’s no program, just dinner. The cafeteria is set up at long banquet tables; if you bring a stroller there’s a parking spot inside the door.

  • Pupusas — revueltas (pork + cheese + beans), queso, frijol con queso, and a vegan option. Tortillas are gluten-free corn.
  • Curtido — the cabbage slaw that goes on top. Homemade, slightly spicy, vinegary.
  • Agua fresca — jamaica or horchata, plus water.
  • Dessert — rotates monthly; June is mango with chamoy.

The tradition

Each grade level hosts twice a school year. The hosting grade brings the masa, the fillings, and the sound system; the PTA covers everything else. This rotation keeps the work distributed and means each family ends up at the long table as a guest more often than they end up behind it as a cook.

If you’d like to host a future month with your child’s grade, sign up below or email volunteer@monroepta.org.

Notes for families

Vegan + gluten-free options available. The cafetería is wheelchair-accessible from the Madrid St entrance. ASL interpretation can be arranged with 48 hours’ notice — email hello@monroepta.org.

A few questions, answered.

If we missed yours, email hello@monroepta.org and we’ll add it here.

01. Do I need to RSVP, or buy a ticket?
No RSVP. The suggested donation is $5 per family, but we never turn anyone away. Cash or Venmo at the door (or skip it — it’s genuinely fine).
02. Is the food free for my child?
Yes. Every Monroe kid eats free at Pupusa Night, regardless of whether their family contributes.
03. I can’t volunteer this month — when’s the next chance?
We hold Pupusa Night the first Tuesday of every month. The host grade rotates, so volunteer needs vary — see the full calendar.
04. What if I have a food allergy?
Tortillas are corn (naturally gluten-free); we always have a vegan filling. For tree-nut, dairy, or other allergies, email hello@monroepta.org 48 hours ahead and we’ll set aside something safe.
05. Can grandparents / aunts / family friends come?
Absolutely. Anyone the family considers family is welcome at the long table. We just ask that all kids are accompanied by a parent or caregiver.
