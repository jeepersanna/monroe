Family Liaisons.

The link between families and the school — three liaisons working in Spanish, Cantonese, and English to make sure every parent has a way in.

Family Liaisons facilitate the conversations that turn parents into partners in their children’s education. They translate paperwork, walk new families through enrollment, and broker the meetings that need brokering — in the language the family is most comfortable speaking.

Call them first. Whether it’s a question about a teacher conference, a confusing district notice, an attendance issue, or just figuring out who to ask — the liaison team is the right starting point.

The role

What a liaison is for

  • Welcome and orient families to the school and the community — familiarizing them with our culture, policies, procedures, and practices.
  • Provide strategies parents can use to promote student achievement at home.
  • Involve families who may not traditionally take part in school activities and events.
  • Provide information to school staff that promotes understanding of students’ families and home cultures.
In practice

Typical things they actually do

  • Connecting families to school professionals and community resources.
  • Setting up parent conferences with teachers or the counselor.
  • Helping families build relationships and lines of communication with the school.
  • Interpretation and translation services — in person, over the phone, and on paperwork.
  • Access to both on-campus and off-campus resources and services.
  • Running family events and workshop series throughout the year.
When in doubt

Call the liaison first.

If you’re unsure who to talk to about something — an absence, a behavior question, an after-school program signup, or a piece of mail you can’t parse — start with the liaison who speaks your family’s language. They’ll take it from there.

For anything urgent, the main office at 415-469-4736 is staffed Monday through Friday during school hours.
