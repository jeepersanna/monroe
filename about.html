Our School

A small school with a big front porch.

Monroe sits on a quiet block of Madrid Street, between a corner taqueria and a row of Edwardians the color of sun-bleached chalk. Kids walk here. They bike here. They show up early to play handball before the bell.

The classrooms are bilingual or trilingual depending on the day, and the hallways carry the smell of arroz con pollo on Tuesdays and dumplings on Fridays. Our murals were painted by students, our garden was built by parents, and our Friday assemblies are the closest thing to a town square the Excelsior has.

We are small — thirteen classrooms, a single play yard — and that is on purpose. Teachers know every student by name. Parents know each other. Fifth graders know the kindergarteners. It is, in the most literal sense, a neighborhood school.

Three Pathways

Choose a language. Find your community.

Every Monroe student belongs to one of three academically aligned pathways. All three pathways share the same core curriculum, the same playground, and the same Friday assembly.

01 · Pathway
雙語浸入

Spanish Two-Way Bilingual Immersion

Inmersión bilingüe dos vías

Native English speakers and native Spanish speakers learn side-by-side, in both languages, from kindergarten on. By fifth grade, students read, write, and argue about books in two languages — and have a second set of friends to do it with.

Grades K–5
Language Mix 50 / 50, EN / ES
Class Size ~22 per room
Learn more →
02 · Pathway
粵語雙文

Cantonese Biliteracy Pathway

Programa bilingüe en cantonés

A heritage-language pathway designed with families who want their children to read, write, and speak Cantonese alongside English. Daily instruction in traditional characters, with culture and history woven through every subject.

Grades K–5
Language Mix ~30% Cantonese
Class Size ~22 per room
Learn more →
03 · Pathway
英語發展

English Language Development

Desarrollo del idioma inglés

Targeted support for multilingual learners, woven through every classroom rather than pulled out from it. Our ELD specialists co-teach, helping kids whose first language is something other than English thrive across all subjects.

Grades All
Model Co-teaching
Languages 15+ home languages
